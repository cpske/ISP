Software Walk-Through

Overview

A walk-through is an informal review of an artifact (work product).  The artifact can be a project document such as the SRS (or a section of the SRS), a technical document like a domain model, source code, a set of UI forms, test cases, etc.

The walk-through should be scheduled in advance and the participants given some time (at least 1 day) to study the artifact they will review.  Usually the author of the artifact requests the walk-through, the project manager helps schedule the walk-through. One participant acts as a recorder to complete the walk-through form.

The result of the walk-through is a list of defects or problems, suggestions, and open issues.  The recorder posts the completed walk-through form on the project management site (Redmine: in Documents section).  The author is responsible for entering bugs and issues into the tracking system; the author may also fix bugs himself, requests someone to fix them, or decides they should not be fixed; or the walk-through team may decide this.

The PMC person is responsible to verify that this procedure is followed.

A PPQA person should check that all items on the walk-through form are actually tracked and resolved (fixed, deferred, determined it is not a bug, etc).

Purpose

(1)  Find defects, including coding errors, ambiguity, incompleteness, standards violations, bad design, or extra features or code that is not required of the spec (these are also defects).

(2)   Share knowledge about an artifact, technology, or how/why something is implemented.

(3)  Obtain feedback about technical quality of the artifact.

Roles

Author

The producer or current owner of the artifact.  The author initiates the request.

After the walk-through, the author is responsible to enter all issues into issue tracking system and see that they are assigned.  He may fix himself, get someone else to fix, defer to later time, or decide not to fix.

Product Manager

Decides whether or not to approve walk-through request; helps choose walk-through team and schedule a time.  PM may also initiate a walk-through request.

Moderator

Moderates the meeting, handles administrative tasks, keep the meeting on track, ensure everyone has fair chance to speak, and results are recorded, and on-time.  The Moderator may be the Author.

Recorder

Completes the walk-through form and posts it on project management site.  Also give to Author.

PMC

Responsible to verify that this process is followed, including recording of issues.  If non-compliance, then take corrective action.  If cannot correct, report to the PM.

PPQA

Responsible to verify that issues are properly addressed and resolved.  For bugs in source code products, test cases should be created to ensure bugs are fixed and don’t occur again (regression testing).

 

How To Do a Walk-Through

  1. Author of an artifact (work product) contacts PM and requests a walk-through.
  2. PM and Author agree on walk-through team, amount of time, and date.
  3. Author arranges meeting, contacts team members, and informs them which artifact (including version!) to review.
  4. Team members familiarize themselves with artifact before walk-through.
  5. At start of walk-through, Recorder completes first part of walk-through template.
  6. Author presents artifact to team, describing its purpose, and leads (“walks”) team through the entire artifact, line-by-line.
  7. The team members ask questions, look for defects, ambiguity, incompleteness, and suggest possible improvements.
  8. Recorder enters the defects and issues on form.
  9. Ending the walk-through: when entire document is reviewed or the scheduled time is up, the moderator should conclude the walk-through. The Recorder enters the amount of times spent on the form.
  10. Debriefing: Moderator gives each person a chance to share his experience.  The purpose is to clear up any tension introduced during meeting, and suggest how to improve future walk-throughs.
  11. Recorder posts the walk-through form on project web site, and gives a copy to the Author.
  12. Author enters issues into tracking system and decides what to do with them (fix, get someone to fix, defer, etc).  Alternatively, the walk-through team can decide this.
  13. PPQA verifies that all issues are adequately resolved and tested.
  14. PMC verifies that this procedure is followed.

Alternative

  1. The walk-through team can decide what to do with each issue/defect instead of the Author deciding this.  This may help in prioritizing and assigning work.

Time Required

Less time than it looks!  Preparation time about 30 minutes per reviewer; actual walk-through time depends on size of artifact, typically 30 minutes – 2 hours.  Walk-throughs can save time by finding defects earlier than testing.
